Posture
Rounded shoulders + tech neck — full correction plan
Root causes · Priority fixes · Daily routine · Desk habits
Root causes of your posture issues
Tight pectorals pulling shoulders forward
Weak rhomboids and rear delts — cannot hold shoulders back
Tight hip flexors from sitting — tilts pelvis forward, ripples up the spine
Weak deep cervical flexors — head drifts forward (forward head posture)
Stiff thoracic spine — upper back locks into a rounded curve
Fix priority order
1. Face pulls — 2x per week minimum
2. Reverse fly machine — 1x per week high volume
3. Chin tucks — morning, evening, and at gym
4. Thoracic foam roller extension — daily at home
5. Pull volume > Push volume
Daily 10-min posture routine — every day including weekends

Desk habits — as important as the gym
Monitor at eye level — if you look down at a screen all day, no amount of gym work fully fixes tech neck. This is the single most impactful non-gym change.
Set a phone alarm every 45 min at work: stand up, do 10 chin tucks, roll shoulders back 5 times. 45 seconds. Breaks the forward-posture pattern.
Sleep on your back or side — not face-down. Face-down sleeping aggressively worsens neck alignment every single night.
Expect 8–12 weeks of consistent effort for visible posture improvement. It took years to develop — months to reverse.
Warm-up
Targeted Warm-Up Strategy
Joint prep · Specificity · Injury prevention
Update: General warm-ups have been removed. Every training day now has a custom 5-minute pre-workout block at the top of its page. This guarantees you are only warming up the specific joints and muscles you are about to load.
Golden rules for machine training
Always adjust seat and pad height before loading weight. 30 seconds of setup prevents months of injury.
Control the eccentric (return/lowering phase) 2–3 seconds on every rep. Muscle is built on the way down, not the lift.
Do not ego-lift. Proper range of motion matters more than the weight stack number. Half-reps produce zero muscle growth signal.
If a machine is occupied: every exercise in this plan has a direct cable machine alternative. Cables are always available and are often superior.
